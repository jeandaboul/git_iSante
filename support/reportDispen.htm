Quality of Care indicators:

Definitions

 

Appropriate Lab Test Indicators

 

Ever had test: Within each 6 month period, the numerator is the number of patients in the categories New + Active who ever had the test of interest. The denominator is the total number of patients in the categories New + Active within the same time periods.

 

Up-to-date on test: Within each 6 month period, the numerator is the number of patients in the categories New + Active who last had the test of interest within the time window to meet the standard of care. The denominator is the total number of patients in the categories New + Active within the same time periods.

 

The time windows to meet the standard of care are defined as follows:

 

§       CD4: For patients on ART, CD4 tests should be administered every 6 months, so the time window to meet the standard of care is 8 months. The time window gives a cushion for real-world considerations that might prohibit a patient from being tested exactly on the 6-month due date. For patients in clinical care (not on ART), CD4 tests should be administered every 12 months, so the time window to meet the standard of care is 14 months.

§       Liver function tests: For patients on ART, liver function tests should be administered every 6 months, so the time window to meet the standard of care is 8 months. For patients in clinical care (not on ART), liver function tests should be administered every 12 months, so the time window to meet the standard of care is 14 months.

§       Hemogram/CBC: For patients both on ART and in clinical care, hemograms should be administered every 3 months, so the time window to meet the standard of care is 5 months.

 

Appropriate Treatment Indicators

 

ART enrollment among medically eligible: Within each 6 month period, the numerator is the number of patients in the categories New + Active newly meeting criteria for enrollment on ART who received ART dispensed within 3 months of meeting criteria. The denominator is the total the number of patients in the categories New + Active newly meeting criteria for enrollment on ART within the same time periods.

 

Medical eligibility criteria for ART enrollment are:

§       CD4<200 : Drawn from Compte CD4 section of the Fiche de la Premiere Visite Clinique, or Résultats section in the Fiche d’Analyses de Laboratoire.

§       TLC<1200 : Drawn from Résultats section in the Fiche d’Analyses de Laboratoire.

 

§       OMS Stade III+CD4<350 : CD4 data drawn from Compte CD4 section of the Fiche de la Premiere Visite Clinique, or Résultats section in the Fiche d’Analyses de Laboratoire ; WHO Stage data drawn from Antécedents Médicaux et Diagnostics Actuels sections of clinical visit forms.

§       OMS Stade IV : WHO Stage data drawn from Antécedents Médicaux et Diagnostics Actuels sections of clinical visit forms.

§       PTME ; Medical Elegibility established at previous visit ; Previous Tritherapy  ; Prophylaxie post-exposition (PEP) : Drawn from medical eligibility question checklist at the end of clinical intake and clinical follow-up forms.

 

 

Cotrimoxizole prophylaxis among medically eligible: Within each 6 month period, the numerator is the number of patients in the categories New + Active newly meeting criteria for Cotrimoxizole prophylaxis for whom this medication was dispensed within 3 months of meeting criteria. The denominator is the total the number of patients in the categories New + Active newly meeting criteria for Cotrimoxizole prophylaxis within the same time periods.

 

For patients on ART, the criteria for Cotrimoxizole prophylaxis are:

§       Any CD4 test result of less than 200

§       Diagnosis of thrush (muguet) in the past 6 months

§       Diagnosis of Oesophagites dûe à candidose in the past 6 months

§       OMS Stage IV

 

For patients in clinical care (never started on ART), the criteria for Cotrimoxizole prophylaxis are:

§       Any CD4 test result of less than 200 in the past 6 months

§       Any diagnosis of thrush (muguet) at any point in the past

§       Any diagnosis of Oesophagites dûe à candidose at any point in the past

§       OMS Stage IV

 

 

Continuation Cotrimoxizole prophylaxis: Within each 6 month period, the numerator is the number of patients in the categories New + Active newly started on Cotrimoxizole within the time window, with at least 1 dispense of Cotrimoxizole within last 2 months of 6 month interval. The denominator is the total the number of patients in the categories New + Active newly started on Cotrimoxizole prophylaxis within the same time periods.

 

 

TB treatment among medically eligible: Within each 6 month period, the numerator is the number of patients in the categories New + Active newly meeting criteria for TB treatment with any 4 of 5 TB drugs (Ethambutol, Isoniazide, Pyrazinamide, Rifampicine, Streptomycine) dispensed within 3 months of meeting criteria. The denominator is the total the number of patients in the categories New + Active newly meeting criteria for TB treatment within the same time periods.

 

Criteria for TB treatment are:

§       Abnormal PPD result, plus one or more of the following:

§       Sputum test done with abnormal findings

§       Chest X-Ray done with abnormal findings

§       Diagnosis of TB (both pulmonary and extrapulmonary/disseminated TB diagnoses are considered)

§       Clinical presentation of TB, defined by:

·      Cough > 3 weeks AND Night sweats AND Fever

OR

·      Cough with expectoration AND Night sweats AND Fever

OR

·      Hemoptysie AND Night sweats AND Fever

 

Continuation TB treatment: Within each 6 month period, the numerator is the number of patients in the categories New + Active newly started on TB treatment within the time window, with at least 1 dispense of at least 2 TB medications within last 2 months of 6 month interval. The denominator is the total the number of patients in the categories New + Active newly started on TB treatment within the same time periods.


  
Incident Disease and Death Reports

 

First diagnosis of PCP among active patients: Within each 6 month period, the numerator is the number of patients in the categories New + Active who received a first diagnosis of PCP within the time window. The first diagnosis date must fall after their clinical intake visit date. The denominator is the number of patients in the categories New + Active within the same time periods.

 

First diagnosis of TB among active patients: Within each 6 month period, the numerator is the number of patients in the categories New + Active who received a first diagnosis of TB (pulmonary or extrapulmonary/disseminated TB) within the time window. The first diagnosis date must fall after their clinical intake visit date. The denominator is the number of patients in the categories New + Active within the same time periods.

 

Deaths by year: The number of patients with discontinuation report completed with death listed as reason for discontinuation. If no date of death is recorded on the discontinuation report, the date considered will be the earliest of the following dates:

·      Recorded date for last patient contact

·      Recorded date for discontinuation from the HIV care and treatment program

·      Date of the Discontinuation Report
